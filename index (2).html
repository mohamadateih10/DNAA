What is DNA?

The Blueprint of Life

DNA, or deoxyribonucleic acid, is the hereditary material that carries genetic instructions essential for the growth, development, functioning, and reproduction of all known living organisms and many viruses.

Found mainly in the cell nucleus, DNA is composed of two strands forming a double helix, built from four nucleotide bases: adenine (A), thymine (T), cytosine (C), and guanine (G).

These sequences encode information that directs the synthesis of proteins, which are critical for cellular structure and function.

Functions of DNA

Genetic Diversity and Evolution

Through processes like replication and mutation, DNA enables inheritance and contributes to genetic diversity, which drives evolution and adaptation in populations over time.

The DNA molecule contains all the information needed to build and maintain an organism. This information is encoded in the sequence of the four nucleotide bases.

When cells divide, DNA is replicated with remarkable accuracy to ensure genetic information is passed to new cells. However, occasional errors in replication lead to mutations, some of which may be beneficial and contribute to evolutionary change.
